The Selection - A Proud Achievement

CHARUSAT Expo 3.0 was a prestigious event that brought together the best innovative projects from across the university. What made this moment truly special for our team was that Mavericks Battlegrounds was the only project selected from the entire IT Department to be showcased at the expo.

This selection was not just a validation of our hard work, but a testament to the quality, innovation, and engagement factor of our game. Among numerous submissions and projects, our multiplayer battle arena game stood out, earning us a spot at one of the most anticipated events of the year.

Setting up our exhibition table was an exciting experience. Our Mavericks Battlegrounds setup became one of the most crowded booths at the expo, with constant queues of eager players waiting for their turn.

Engaging with 5000+ Visitors

The response was overwhelming! Over the course of the expo, we had the privilege of interacting with:

  • School Students: Young gamers who brought fresh energy and enthusiasm, trying out different strategies and characters
  • College Students: Fellow developers and gaming enthusiasts who appreciated the technical aspects and gameplay mechanics
  • Faculty Members: Professors and staff who were impressed by the project's scope and educational value
  • Industry Visitors: Professionals who provided valuable insights into game development best practices

Each interaction was unique and valuable. We observed players' reactions in real-time, noted their strategies, and engaged in countless discussions about game design, development challenges, and future enhancements.

Valuable Feedback and Suggestions

üí° Key Feedback Themes
  • Gameplay Balance: Suggestions for character balancing and power-up distribution
  • Visual Enhancements: Recommendations for improved visual effects and UI/UX design
  • New Features: Ideas for additional game modes, maps, and character abilities
  • Mobile Version: Strong demand for a mobile-optimized version of the game
  • Tutorial System: Request for better onboarding for new players
  • Multiplayer Experience: Suggestions for enhanced online multiplayer features

The feedback we received was incredibly constructive and actionable. Players didn't just tell us what they liked‚Äîthey shared specific suggestions on how we could make the game even better. We collected feedback through:

  • Direct conversations during gameplay sessions
  • Written feedback forms at our booth
  • Video recordings of gameplay with player commentary
  • Follow-up discussions via social media and email

Implementing the Changes

The expo wasn't just about showcasing our work‚Äîit was a learning experience that will directly shape the future of Mavericks Battlegrounds. Based on the feedback received, we're now working on:

  • Refining character abilities and balance based on observed gameplay patterns
  • Improving the tutorial system to better onboard new players
  • Enhancing visual effects and polishing the user interface
  • Exploring feasibility of a mobile version to reach a wider audience
  • Adding new game modes suggested by the community
  • Optimizing multiplayer performance based on connection feedback

What This Experience Taught Us

CHARUSAT Expo 3.0 was more than just an exhibition‚Äîit was a masterclass in understanding player psychology, gathering user feedback, and iterating on game design. Key learnings include:

  • User Testing is Invaluable: Watching real players interact with your game reveals issues you never considered
  • Diverse Perspectives Matter: Different age groups and skill levels approach the game differently
  • Community Building: Direct interaction with players helps build a loyal community
  • Iterative Development: No game is ever "finished"‚Äîplayer feedback drives continuous improvement
  • Communication Skills: Explaining technical concepts to non-technical audiences is a valuable skill
